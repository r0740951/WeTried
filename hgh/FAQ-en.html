Frequently asked questions

Here do you find some questions that we get a lot.

  • Can everybody take our goods?
    Yes, everyone who belongs to the human race may take our goods. If you are poor or rich, working, going to school, Belgian, foreigner, everybody is welcome. Pets are also allowed as long as they don't disturbe the other customers.
  • Can you take as much as you want?
    Yes, there are no boundaries on it but we ask to show respect for the work of the volunteers and the other customers. Choose wisely, "the world has enough for everyone's need, but not enough for everyone's greed." - Mahatma Gandhi
  • Are you allowed to resell the goods to others?
    Yes but we are convinced that reselling used goods doesn't happen out of greed but out of necessity. Loans and welfare are many times not high enough to pay the rising costs. Bills can only be paid in money, not in goods. We try not to jugde about others and we ask the same kind of respect from our customers.
  • Which goods can be brought in and which ones not?
    We maintain our rights to reject goods that are too big, broken or incomplete or where we have a surplus off.
  • Is food allowed?
    That is not the purpose, it's about goods. But treats for our volunteers are allowed.
  • When can I bring in some goods?
    During the opening hours. It is also allowed to bring in some goods when the volunteers are present.
  • Do you pick up our goods?
    No, we work with volunteers and we don't have the time and recources for it.
  • How do you make money if you give everything away for free?
    Clothes/shoes which are not wereable, old metals, broken electronics are delivered to recycling companies. They give us a compensation for that. Also our volunteers work on festivals, they organise sales drives, christmas markets, ... . Gifts are always welcome for our efforts and we hope that pleasant people start an action for Music for Life.

Route description
From Geel

If you are coming from Geel, you follow the Ringlaan N71 until you are over the bridje of the Beverlo channel. Once over the bridge and past the traffic circle, you take the first left and you drive in the Martinus Van Gurplaan. After about 800 meters, you can find our donation store on the right side of the street.

From Neerpelt/Overpelt

If you are coming from Neerpelt and you want to get to the donation store, follow the Ringlaan N71. Once you are past the crossing point with the N769 to Luyksgestel, you stay on the N71 for about 1.9 kilometers. After this you turn right in the Martinus van Gurplaan. Once you are in this street, you can find the donation store after 800 meters on the rightside of the street.

Parking?

You can park at the cross point with the Martinus van Gurplaan and the Dalstraat on gravel road. Cars with trailers can also be parked at this place.

Public transport?
Bus
  • Bus 84 Geel - Hamont. (This bus has stops in Hamont, Lommel, Mol and Geel). You need to get off on the stop "Werkplaatsen Dubbelrij". From here it is a five minute walk.
  • Belbus 713 Lommel West, this bus has stops in Heeserbergen, Heuvel, Lommel, Werkplaatsen and Wijerwerken. Get off at the stop werkplaatsen kerk.
Train
  • Lommel Station is about 20 minutes by bicycle and 1 hour by food.
  • At the station you can take the bus 58 to Beringen- Lommel. You need to get off at the stop Lommel Kerk. Then you take bus 84 to Hamont - Geel and you get off at the stop Werkplaatsen Dubbelrij. Overall you are about 25 minutes along the way.
