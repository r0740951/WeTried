A part of our history

Annemarie Kota got the question if she had ever heard of a give-away shop. This question was asked with the closure of her green grosery in Olmen-Balen. A give-away shop is a store where people can bring and get goods for free. Annemarie had never heard about the concept of a give-away store before.

To get inspired she looked at the shop "Het Huisje van Overvloed" in Leuven. Full of ideas she returned to Balen and set up a give-away shop with her husband and a group of volunteers at home. The shop was called give-away shop Olmen. The official opening followed in September 2012

After a succesful first year, the give-away shop needed to move. Gife-away shop Olmen closed its doors but nowadays it still is an actual association that organizes an annual give-away festival in Olmen.

A call via newspapers and social media for a new location for the giveaway, was answered by the VZW Parochiale Werken in Lommel. They immediately got an old empty school building available to house the new store. One of the conditions was the establishment of a non-profit organization.

This resulted in the foundation of non-profit organization 'Het Gouden Hart' in June 2014. That summer we worked hard on the design of the new store at the Martinus Van Gurplaan in Lommel. And on 30 August 2014 the new store was officially opened.

Our team

The core of our team consists of 15 volunteers. They are commited to the smooth operation of the giveaway store. A few tasks are listed below:

  • Welcoming the customers
  • Receiving their goods
  • Sorting them
  • Put them in the store
  • Cleanup
  • Take part of activities
  • ...
